Synopsis

The past has changed, but like a fiery comet, he still comes. Hwa Moogi, the one without equal, slashed through Murim's skies with his sword, toppling every lord who dared to face him — even Cheonma, the father of Geom Mugeuk. But Mugeuk didn't accept defeat. Instead, he turned to the forbidden: a time-bending ritual that hurled him into his own past. Now, retracing his steps, he races against the clock until Hwa Moogi emerges once again. But this time, it is not just about power — it is about mending old wounds and forging new bonds to unite his people and prepare for the bloodtide to come.

Why You Should Read It

Absolute Regression stands apart from nearly every other regression manhwa because its protagonist's return to the past is driven not by a desire to accumulate power but by a genuine longing to fix broken relationships and protect the people he loves. Geom Mugeuk is one of the most uniquely written protagonists in the genre — warm, witty, and emotionally intelligent in a way that makes every conversation he has feel genuinely compelling. The father-son dynamic between Mugeuk and the Heavenly Demon is widely praised as one of the most heartwarming relationships in any manhwa. Combined with rich murim worldbuilding, sharp pacing, and some of the best character writing the genre has to offer, this is a must-read for anyone who wants a regression story with real emotional depth.

Major Themes

  • Regret and the desire to mend what was broken
  • Family bonds and the relationship between father and son
  • Revenge tempered by compassion and wisdom
  • Unity and the cost of leading a people through crisis
  • Identity — who you become when revenge is no longer enough

Characters

  1. Geom Mugeuk — Main Protagonist

    The second son of the Heavenly Demon and the sole person to have used the Great Regression Technique to return to the past. Tall, handsome, with long dark hair tied back by a red ribbon, Mugeuk is a deep thinker who handles every situation with finesse and no wasted energy. Unlike most regression protagonists who are cold and calculating, Mugeuk is warm, talkative, and endlessly sincere — talking, joking, and looking into the souls of everyone around him. His greatest motivation is rebuilding the bond with his father that he neglected in his previous life, and using that foundation to prepare his people for the coming storm.

  2. Geom Woojin — The Heavenly Demon, Mugeuk's Father

    The Cheonma — the supreme leader of the Heavenly Demon Divine Cult and one of the most powerful martial artists in the world. A man of few words who rarely expresses his feelings openly, yet clearly cares deeply for his sons. In Mugeuk's previous life the two were distant and barely knew each other. In this life, thanks to Mugeuk's persistent efforts to connect, their relationship grows into one of the most beloved dynamics in the story. A proud tsundere father who grows stronger even in this second life, thanks to the warmth his son brings back into his world.

  3. Hwa Moogi — Main Antagonist, The Everlasting Martial God

    The greatest martial artist of the era, titled the Everlasting Martial God and the Greatest of All Time. Hwa Moogi single-handedly toppled the three great factions of the murim world — the Righteous Path Alliance, the Evil Path Alliance, and the Heavenly Demon Divine Cult — and unified the martial world under his iron rule. He is the reason Mugeuk regressed, and the looming threat that every decision Mugeuk makes in the present life is ultimately preparing to face. A terrifyingly powerful antagonist whose presence shadows the entire story even before he appears.

  4. Seo Dae-ryong — Mugeuk's Right Hand

    Mugeuk's most trusted subordinate and closest companion within the Heavenly Demon Divine Cult, described as the protagonist's right arm. He appears from the very beginning and is present in almost every arc related to the inside of the cult. Initially carrying a cynical and guarded personality, his time with Mugeuk gradually transforms him into someone brighter, more open, and prone to joking alongside his lord. He eventually rises to become the head of Hwangcheongak, one of the cult's most important positions, a testament to how much he grows throughout the story.

Adaptations

No anime or drama adaptation has been announced as of 2026. The manhwa is currently ongoing on Naver Webtoon and Webtoon (English), released every Saturday.

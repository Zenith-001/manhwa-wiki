Synopsis

Excellent grades, good looks, above average fighting skills, and a sense of justice. Kim Hyun-sung seemingly had everything going for him as a model first-grade student. But after helping a bullied classmate, he became the target of Park Min-cheol's gang — the Golden Circle — and suffered relentless school violence. On the eve of his graduation he was called to the rooftop and pushed off, left in a vegetative state for ten years with only his hearing and consciousness intact. Unable to die, he endured a decade of hell listening to his family suffer and his tormentors thrive. Then one day, he suddenly returns to the day the bullying began — and this time, he has decided to become the malice that wipes out the evil.

Why You Should Read It

Devil Returns to School Days is one of the most gripping and emotionally charged school-life revenge manhwa ever written. What sets it apart is the sheer weight of Kim Hyun-sung's suffering — ten years of conscious paralysis gives his desire for revenge a depth and justification that most protagonists simply don't have. His revenge is not reckless or hot-blooded; it is methodical, intelligent, and deeply satisfying. The manhwa tackles the dark realities of school violence, corruption, and complicity with unflinching honesty while keeping the story propulsive and entertaining. By the same author as The Heavenly Demon Can't Live a Normal Life, it carries the same sharp writing and strong character work that fans of that series will immediately recognize.

Major Themes

  • Revenge as justice against systemic evil and corruption
  • The long-lasting trauma of school violence and bullying
  • Complicity — those who watch and do nothing
  • Resilience and the will to survive the unimaginable
  • The thin line between justice and becoming what you hate

Characters

  1. Kim Hyun-sung — Main Protagonist

    A first-grade model student with excellent grades, a handsome appearance, above-average fighting skills, and a strong sense of justice. After helping a bullied classmate, he became the target of the Golden Circle gang, was pushed off a rooftop, and spent ten years in a vegetative state — fully conscious but unable to move. Returning to the day the bullying began, he abandons his former righteousness and embraces becoming the devil that will systematically dismantle every person responsible for destroying his life. He is often depicted with a devilish smirk that has become one of the most iconic expressions in the manhwa.

  2. Park Min-cheol — Main Antagonist, Leader of the Golden Circle

    The leader of the Golden Circle, the school's most powerful and feared bully gang and the primary source of school violence that ruined Hyun-sung's life. Entitled, manipulative, and protected by his family's wealth and connections, Min-cheol represents the kind of systemic evil that hides behind privilege. His downfall at the hands of Hyun-sung forms the core revenge arc of the story and is among the most cathartic payoffs in the genre.

  3. Lee Jeong-min — Bullied Classmate, Catalyst

    The classmate whose bullying Hyun-sung tried to stop, which made him a direct target of the Golden Circle. Lee Jeong-min is the spark that set Hyun-sung's tragedy in motion and remains an important emotional anchor throughout the story. In the regression timeline, Hyun-sung's approach to protecting him changes completely — less idealistic, more calculated, and far more effective.

  4. Kim Hyun-cheol — Hyun-sung's Older Brother

    Hyun-sung's older brother who spent the ten years of Hyun-sung's vegetative state caring for him and their family under enormous hardship. During his years of paralysis, Hyun-sung could hear everything around him, including the sacrifices his brother made — making the bond between them one of the most emotionally resonant aspects of the story. Protecting his brother and giving his family back a better life is one of Hyun-sung's most personal motivations beyond pure revenge.

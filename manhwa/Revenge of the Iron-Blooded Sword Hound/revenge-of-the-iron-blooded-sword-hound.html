Synopsis

Raised to kill like a pack of loyal hounds, the Baskerville children took countless lives as a band of fearsome assassins. Despite being the shunned illegitimate son of the brood, Vikir served his family faithfully, only to meet his untimely demise at his father's hands — beheaded at the guillotine under false accusations of consorting with demons. But when he is inexplicably reborn back into his infantile body, Vikir, armed with the memories and knowledge of his past life, swears to rise above the pack and take his revenge. This time, he is ready to bite the hand that feeds.

Why You Should Read It

Revenge of the Iron-Blooded Sword Hound stands out in the regression genre for its dark, methodical tone and a protagonist who is genuinely cold and calculating rather than cheerful or idealistic. Vikir's revenge is not a hot-blooded rampage but a slow, strategic dismantling of everyone who wronged him, which makes each victory feel earned and satisfying. The story also layers a larger demon-hunting conspiracy on top of the personal revenge narrative, giving the world depth beyond just one man's grudge. The worldbuilding is rich and the supporting cast — particularly Dolores — adds real emotional complexity. It is one of the most gripping dark fantasy manhwa currently serializing.

Major Themes

  • Betrayal and the price of blind loyalty
  • Revenge as a purpose and its emotional cost
  • Power and what it means to truly earn it
  • Family — both the bonds that protect and the ones that destroy
  • Identity — between being a weapon and being a person

Characters

  1. Vikir Van Baskerville — Main Protagonist

    The illegitimate son of the Baskerville family who devoted his entire life to serving his clan, only to be falsely accused and executed at the guillotine. Reborn into his infant body with all memories intact, he sets out on a cold and calculated path of revenge. Unlike most regression protagonists, Vikir is reserved, emotionless on the surface, and entirely focused on dismantling his enemies one by one. His growth from a betrayed hound into a force that even the demons of the world must reckon with forms the backbone of the story.

  2. Hugo Le Baskerville — Patriarch of Baskerville, Vikir's Father

    The iron-fisted head of the Baskerville clan and one of the seven pillars supporting the Rok Empire. Hugo is a Sword Master known as the Sword Star, a man of absolute resolve who values strength above all else and treats even his own children as weapons to be sharpened. His complicated relationship with Vikir — part contempt, part grudging pride — is one of the most nuanced dynamics in the story. He is simultaneously the object of Vikir's revenge and one of the most compelling figures in the narrative.

  3. Dolores L. Quovadis — Saintess, Key Ally

    The Saintess of the Rune Order and daughter of the powerful Quovadis clan, one of the seven great noble families of the empire. Dolores is principled, compassionate, and fiercely intelligent, serving as the president of Colosseo Academy's student council. She initially distrusts Vikir but grows to admire his resilience and moral fortitude as they repeatedly cross paths in their separate fights against demonic corruption. She is widely regarded as one of the best supporting characters in the manhwa.

  4. Camus Morgue — Rival, Love Interest

    A prodigiously talented member of House Morgue, one of the seven great noble families, and one of Vikir's most persistent rivals throughout their time at Colosseo Academy. Ambitious, intense, and fiercely proud of her lineage, Camus shares a complex relationship with Vikir defined by rivalry, mutual respect, and deep-seated feelings she rarely shows openly. Her loyalty to Vikir, even when he disappears and is presumed dead, is one of the most emotionally resonant arcs in the later story.

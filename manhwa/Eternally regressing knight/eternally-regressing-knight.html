Synopsis

"You're a genius." Those words he heard as a child were poison. Encrid dreamed of becoming a knight, but he soon realized it was futile. "You want to live by the sword with that level of skill?" Some laughed at him. "Just give up," others advised. Yet his dream never wavered. He slept less, ran more, and trained harder. One day, he died — stabbed in the neck on the battlefield. Then Encrid opened his eyes again to the same morning. Cursed by a mysterious amulet that grants his wish in the cruelest way possible, he is doomed to relive the same brutal day over and over, dying each time he hits a wall he cannot overcome. Rather than despair, Encrid chooses to treat each loop as another chance to grow stronger — one agonizing repetition at a time.

Why You Should Read It

Eternally Regressing Knight is one of the most refreshing entries in the regression genre because its protagonist has absolutely no innate talent. Encrid earns every single improvement through sheer persistence, pain, and an unbreakable refusal to give up — making his growth feel more satisfying than almost any other manhwa protagonist. The story is also celebrated for its outstanding supporting cast, a squad of memorable and deeply loyal comrades whose bond with Encrid is one of the most heartwarming dynamics in the genre. It balances brutal combat with genuine humor and emotional depth, and its slower pacing feels earned rather than slow. Frequently compared to Re:Zero but with a far more likable main character, it is a must-read for any fan of action and psychological manhwa.

Major Themes

  • Perseverance in the face of impossible odds
  • The value of hard work over natural talent
  • Loyalty and the bonds forged through shared suffering
  • The psychological toll of endless repetition and death
  • Finding purpose and joy even within a curse

Characters

  1. Encrid — Main Protagonist

    A 30-year-old foot soldier with no natural talent who dreams of becoming a knight despite being mocked by everyone around him. After being cursed by a magical amulet that resurrects him each time he dies, he treats the loop not as a punishment but as an opportunity to train endlessly. Driven by pure determination and a refusal to quit, he slowly breaks through wall after wall that would have broken any other man. His maniacal smile whenever he overcomes a challenge has become one of the most iconic character traits in the manhwa.

  2. Rem — Squad Member, Closest Companion

    A beast-type fighter and one of Encrid's most trusted squadmates. Rem possesses the Heart of the Beast ability, which he eventually teaches to Encrid, one of the most pivotal moments in the story. Despite his fierce exterior, Rem's loyalty to Encrid is absolute and unwavering across every single loop. His dynamic with Encrid — equal parts camaraderie and exasperation — is one of the most beloved relationships in the manhwa.

  3. Esther — Powerful Mage, Ally

    An extraordinarily powerful mage of unknown age who is herself bound by a curse and claims to stay with Encrid only in order to be freed from it. She takes the form of a panther for most of the story, only revealing her human form to Encrid alone — and killing anyone else who dares look at her. Her true motivations and the depth of her attachment to Encrid unfold slowly over the course of the series, making her one of its most intriguing and mysterious characters.

  4. Jabel — Squad Member, Berserker

    A berserker-type fighter and another core member of Encrid's squad. Jabel is wild, chaotic, and terrifyingly effective in battle, bringing a raw and unhinged energy to the group that balances Encrid's more measured approach. Like the rest of the squad, his loyalty to Encrid grows naturally through the shared experiences of combat and survival, even though he is unaware of the loops that Encrid endures alone.

Adaptations

No anime or drama adaptation has been announced as of 2026. The manhwa is currently ongoing on Naver Webtoon with a highly dedicated international fanbase.

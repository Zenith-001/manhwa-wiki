About

Brandon Lee, writing under the pen name TurtleMe, is a Korean-American fantasy novelist and webcomic author born on May 29, 1993 in South Korea. He emigrated to the United States at age 3 and was primarily raised in Los Angeles before eventually settling in Kirkland, a suburb near Seattle, Washington, where he currently lives with his wife Grace and their Pomeranian dog, Roy. He graduated from UC Berkeley with a major in Psychology and went on to work in finance — a career he described as increasingly dreadful — before making the leap into full-time writing.

He began writing The Beginning After the End (TBATE) as a personal creative outlet while still working at his corporate job, first serializing it on the web fiction platform Royalroad starting October 28, 2015. The story found a growing audience and eventually migrated to Tapas in January 2017. In July 2018, a webtoon adaptation illustrated by Indonesian artist Fuyuki23 began on Tapas, which was specifically created for the North American market. The series became one of the most commercially successful English-language web novels and webcomics ever produced, accumulating over 60 million views worldwide and generating more than $500,000 per month as a webtoon at its peak. Yen Press began publishing the webtoon in print, with volume 10 releasing in February 2026. The original web novel concluded on August 24, 2025 after 529 chapters, running for a total of 10 years.

In 2025, The Beginning After the End became the first English web novel and webcomic to be adapted into an anime, produced by Studio A-Cat and streaming on Crunchyroll. A second season premiered in April 2026. TurtleMe has been notably transparent about his experience with the anime production, acknowledging on Reddit that navigating the anime industry — where creators have limited say in adaptation decisions — was a significant learning experience for him.

Writing Style & Themes

TurtleMe's writing is defined by a rare and deliberate blending of Eastern and Western storytelling traditions. Having grown up surrounded by both manhwa, manga, and xianxia novels on one hand and Western fantasy epics like Eragon and The Lord of the Rings on the other, he crafted The Beginning After the End as a bridge between those two worlds — familiar enough to both audiences to feel welcoming, yet fresh enough in its combination to feel genuinely original. He has cited Fullmetal Alchemist and Hunter x Hunter as key influences, drawn to stories that challenge characters on a psychological and emotional level rather than simply through power escalation.

His central thematic focus is character growth through relationships and adversity. The Beginning After the End is at its heart a reincarnation story about a former king trying to find purpose in a new life, and TurtleMe uses the early childhood and family arcs — sections that most isekai stories skip over entirely — to build a deeply invested emotional foundation before the action begins. He has spoken about how Arthur Leywin's growth across multiple phases of life, and the bonds formed along the way, are what he considers the true core of the series rather than the power progression that tends to draw in casual readers.

About

Soul Pung is a South Korean web novel author best known for Eternally Regressing Knight (오늘만 사는 기사), a dark fantasy action series that began serialization in 2023 and has grown into one of the most beloved ongoing manhwa and web novel properties in the genre. Like most Korean web novelists of his generation, Soul Pung maintains a very private public profile — his real name, background, and personal details have not been publicly disclosed.

Eternally Regressing Knight follows Enkrid, a soldier with no innate talent who dreams of becoming a knight. Cursed to relive the same day over and over whenever he dies, he uses each repeated cycle to build his skills purely through accumulated muscle memory, instinct, and sheer stubborn will. The novel proved enormously popular on the Korean web novel platform, eventually being adapted into a manhwa illustrated by Leean and serialized on Naver Webtoon, with the adaptation written by Lee Hyun-min and Ga Nara. As of 2026 the web novel has surpassed 920 chapters and remains ongoing, while the manhwa adaptation continues to attract a growing international readership through platforms such as AsuraScans.

Beyond Eternally Regressing Knight, Soul Pung has also written Becoming the Monarch and The Perfect Hybrid, further demonstrating a consistent interest in fantasy action with strong character development at its core. His ability to sustain momentum and quality across hundreds of chapters has earned him recognition as one of the most prolific and reliable authors currently active in the Korean web novel space.

Writing Style & Themes

Soul Pung's writing is defined by a deep respect for the value of effort over talent. Eternally Regressing Knight deliberately inverts the standard fantasy power fantasy — instead of a gifted protagonist who rises effortlessly, Enkrid is a man whose body is described as perfectly suited for farming rather than combat. His only advantages are an unbreakable will and a time loop curse that allows him to accumulate muscle memory, reflexes, and instincts across repeated deaths. This makes every small victory feel genuinely hard-won and earned in a way that is rare in the genre.

Soul Pung's pacing is one of his most praised qualities. He knows precisely how many regression cycles to show the reader to convey progress without becoming repetitive, and he varies the obstacles Enkrid faces — it is never simply a matter of fighting a stronger enemy — keeping the story fresh across hundreds of chapters. The squad dynamic between Enkrid and his dysfunctional but deeply capable squadmates is another standout element, with each supporting character carrying their own distinct personality, fighting style, and personal arc. The result is a story that feels less like a solo protagonist fantasy and more like a war story built around unlikely bonds forged under extreme pressure.

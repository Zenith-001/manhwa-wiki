About

BK_Moon, whose real name is Baek-kyung Moon (문백경), is a South Korean web novel author best known for The Greatest Estate Developer (역대급 영지 설계사), a fantasy comedy isekai series that became one of the most popular and entertaining manhwa on Naver Webtoon. His early published works were in the romance genre, and because of that many readers initially assumed he was female — a misconception he has since been identified as finding quietly amusing. He is known to own six cats, an affection that has made its way into his fiction in the form of cat-inspired characters and creatures appearing across multiple works.

The Greatest Estate Developer was serialized on Naver Web Novel from June 3, 2019 to November 12, 2020. A manhwa adaptation adapted by Lee Hyun-min and illustrated by Kim Hyun-soo began on Naver Webtoon on August 5, 2021, and as of March 2026 the webtoon has reached over 220 episodes including spin-off chapters. Seven collected volumes have been published as of November 2025. The series has been widely praised for its humor, creative use of modern engineering knowledge in a fantasy setting, and its surprisingly warm emotional core built around family and loyalty.

One of the most notable aspects of BK_Moon's body of work is that all of his major novels share the same fictional universe, each set at different points in the same world's history. What a Bountiful Harvest, Demon Lord! is set in ancient times before the events of The Greatest Estate Developer. I Became the Chef of the Dragon King is a prequel set some time before TGED and is directly referenced within it. The Crown Prince That Sells Medicine is a sequel set after the events of TGED. The Apothecary Prince is set roughly three centuries after TGED and contains significant spoilers for that story. This interconnected approach to worldbuilding has given BK_Moon a devoted readership that follows his entire output rather than just individual titles.

Writing Style & Themes

BK_Moon's greatest strength as a writer is his ability to balance genuinely funny comedy with real emotional warmth without either undermining the other. The Greatest Estate Developer is one of the funniest manhwa ever written — the protagonist's shameless scheming, deadpan expressions, and refusal to play by the rules of the noble world he has been dropped into generate consistent laughs — yet the story also develops a deeply felt family dynamic and a genuine sense of stakes as the series progresses. This tonal balance is difficult to achieve and rarer still to sustain across hundreds of chapters.

Thematically, BK_Moon is drawn to stories about people finding purpose and belonging in worlds that were not designed for them. His protagonists tend to be outsiders who apply unconventional knowledge or approaches to problems that conventional thinking cannot solve, and who build loyal communities around themselves not through raw power but through demonstrated capability and genuine care. His interconnected universe approach also reflects a deep investment in worldbuilding — each new series adds texture and history to the same world, rewarding readers who follow his full body of work with a richer experience than any single title can provide alone.

About

Chugong is a South Korean web novel author who rose to global prominence with Solo Leveling, one of the most widely read and beloved manhwa series in history. Despite the enormous worldwide reach of his work, Chugong remains an intensely private figure — his real name has never been publicly disclosed and almost nothing is known about his personal life or background. He is known to occasionally share lore, trivia, and answers to fan questions in Korean online communities such as DCInside, where readers have compiled his comments into extensive archives over the years.

Solo Leveling began serialization on KakaoPage in 2016 as a web novel under the title 나 혼자만 레벨업 (Na Honjaman Level Up) and ran until 2018. The story of Sung Jin-woo, a weak hunter who gains a mysterious leveling system, quickly captured the imaginations of millions of readers in Korea and eventually the entire world. The web novel was adapted into a manhwa illustrated by Dubu of Redice Studio, which launched in 2018 and concluded in 2021. Both the novel and manhwa went on to become one of the most commercially successful manhwa properties ever produced, spawning an anime series by A-1 Pictures in 2024, a sequel titled Solo Leveling: Ragnarok, a Netflix live-action adaptation announced in 2025, and multiple video game adaptations.

Beyond Solo Leveling, Chugong also wrote I Saw the Overlord (패왕을 보았다), another web novel published on KakaoPage. He has expressed support for Solo Leveling: Ragnarok, the official sequel written by a different author under the name Daul, saying the sequel evoked the same emotions in him that he felt when he first began serializing the original story.

Writing Style & Themes

Chugong's writing is defined by its relentless pace, clean prose, and a deeply satisfying power fantasy structure. Solo Leveling follows a clear and deliberate progression arc — the protagonist begins at the absolute bottom and systematically rises to the top — and Chugong executes this formula with a precision and momentum that few authors have matched. His writing is action-forward with minimal filler, prioritising the excitement of each battle and level-up over extended character introspection or complex political worldbuilding.

Thematically, Solo Leveling is driven by themes of perseverance in the face of weakness, the burden of solitude, and the emotional weight of protecting the people you love. Sung Jin-woo begins as someone ridiculed and dismissed by the world around him, and his journey from powerless to all-powerful resonates deeply with readers who have felt overlooked or underestimated. Chugong himself has noted in fan interactions that certain characters — such as the unpredictable antagonist Kang Taeshik — were genuinely enjoyable to write because they moved naturally and organically within the story without needing excessive planning.

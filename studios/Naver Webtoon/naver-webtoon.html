Overview

Naver Webtoon, now operating globally under the brand WEBTOON, is the largest and most influential manhwa and webcomic platform in the world. Originally launched in 2004 as an in-house service of Naver Corporation — South Korea's biggest internet company — it pioneered the vertical scroll format that is now the global standard for digital comics. The platform is home to some of the most beloved manhwa titles ever published, including Tower of God, True Beauty, Noblesse, and Lookism, and has played a defining role in bringing Korean comics to international audiences.

In May 2017, Naver spun off its webtoon service into a dedicated subsidiary called Naver Webtoon Corporation. In 2021 it acquired the web fiction platform Wattpad for USD 600 million, significantly expanding its global content ecosystem. In June 2024, the company listed on Nasdaq as Webtoon Entertainment Inc., becoming one of the first Korean digital content companies to go public on a US stock exchange. In September 2025, The Walt Disney Company acquired a 2% equity stake in Webtoon Entertainment as part of an expanded content collaboration.

Today WEBTOON operates in over 100 countries, supports creators through its Canvas self-publishing platform, and has become the gateway through which millions of readers outside Korea discovered manhwa for the first time.

History

  • 2004 — Naver launches its online webtoon service, introducing the vertical scroll format to Korean readers.
  • 2013 — Naver introduces the Profit Page Share program allowing creators to earn revenue through the platform.
  • 2014 — Line Webtoon launches globally, bringing Korean webtoons to international audiences in English for the first time.
  • 2017 — Naver spins off its webtoon business into an independent subsidiary, Naver Webtoon Corporation. Studio N is established to produce dramas based on webtoon IPs.
  • 2019 — Line Webtoon rebrands to simply WEBTOON in English-speaking markets.
  • 2021 — Naver acquires Wattpad for USD 600 million, merging two of the world's largest digital storytelling platforms.
  • 2024 — Webtoon Entertainment Inc. lists on Nasdaq in June. The Walt Disney Company acquires a 2% equity stake in September 2025.
  • 2026 — Yongsoo Kim appointed as President of Webtoon Entertainment in March 2026.

Business Model

Naver Webtoon operates primarily on a free-to-read model supported by advertising revenue, making its vast library of titles accessible to anyone with an internet connection. To monetize individual chapters ahead of their free release schedule, the platform uses a virtual currency system where readers can purchase coins to unlock episodes early — a model known as "Fast Pass." Creators on the platform earn through a revenue sharing system tied to ad income and reader engagement.

The platform also operates Canvas, a self-publishing section where amateur and independent creators can publish their work for free, giving it one of the most diverse content libraries of any webtoon platform. Webtoon Originals, the platform's premium content banner, features professionally produced series with higher production values and exclusive content. The company also generates significant revenue through IP licensing, with numerous titles being adapted into anime, Korean dramas, films, and games.

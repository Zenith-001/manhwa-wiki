Manhwa Studio

Manhwa studios and publishing platforms are the backbone of the industry. They are responsible for discovering talent, publishing titles, and distributing manhwa to readers worldwide. Some operate as traditional publishing houses while others are purely digital platforms. Over the years these studios have shaped the direction of manhwa as an art form and played a major role in taking it global. This page covers the five most prominent studios in the manhwa industry.

Naver Webtoon

The platform that brought manhwa to the world.

  • Founded: 2004
  • Country: South Korea
  • Known For: Tower of God, True Beauty, Noblesse, Lookism

Naver Webtoon, originally launched as Naver Comics in 2004, is the largest and most influential manhwa platform in the world. It was founded as part of Naver Corporation, South Korea's biggest internet company, and quickly became the go-to destination for webcomic readers across the country. The platform pioneered the vertical scroll webtoon format that is now standard across the entire industry. In 2014 it launched its global English platform, bringing thousands of Korean titles to international readers for the first time officially. Today Naver Webtoon boasts over 170 million monthly users worldwide and has been instrumental in launching some of the most beloved manhwa titles ever created.

Kakao Entertainment

Home to some of manhwa's biggest blockbusters.

  • Founded: 2021(merger of Kakao M and Kakao Page)
  • Country: South Korea
  • Known For: Solo Leveling, The God of High School, Itaewon Class

Kakao Entertainment was formed in 2021 through the merger of Kakao M and Kakao Page, two already powerful forces in the Korean entertainment and digital content industry. Its webtoon arm, operating through the Kakao Webtoon platform, is home to some of the most commercially successful manhwa titles ever published. Solo Leveling, one of the most read manhwa in history, was published through Kakao's platform and went on to receive a globally acclaimed anime adaptation. Beyond manhwa, Kakao Entertainment is a major player in K-pop, Korean dramas, and film, making it one of the most powerful entertainment conglomerates in Asia. Its global reach continues to expand through partnerships and international platform launches.

Lezhin Comics

Pushing the boundaries of mature manhwa storytelling.

  • Founded: 2013
  • Country: South Korea
  • Known For: BJ Alex, Killing Stalking, Pearl Boy

Lezhin Comics was founded in 2013 with a focus on premium, paid digital comics content targeting adult readers. Unlike Naver and Kakao which rely heavily on free ad-supported models, Lezhin operates on a coin-based payment system where readers purchase chapters individually. This model allowed the platform to publish more mature and niche content that wouldn't fit the mainstream free platforms. Lezhin quickly built a dedicated global fanbase, particularly in North America, for its mature romance, thriller, and psychological manhwa titles. It launched its English platform in 2015 and became one of the first Korean webtoon platforms to successfully monetize international audiences directly. Despite some controversies over creator payment disputes in its early years, Lezhin remains one of the most recognized names in the mature manhwa space.

Tapas Media

Bringing webtoons to the English-speaking world.

  • Founded: 2012
  • Country: United States
  • Known For: SubZero, Cursed Princess Club, Midnight Poppy Land

Tapas Media, originally launched as Tapastic in 2012, is a US-based webtoon platform that played a crucial role in introducing the webtoon format to English-speaking audiences. Unlike the Korean platforms, Tapas was built from the ground up for an international audience, allowing both amateur and professional creators to publish their work. This open publishing model helped it build a massive library of English-language webtoons alongside translated Korean titles. In 2022 Tapas was acquired by Kakao Entertainment, bringing it under the same umbrella as some of Korea's biggest manhwa properties. Tapas has been the launchpad for several titles that gained huge fanbases, particularly in the romance and fantasy genres, and continues to be an important platform for webtoon creators targeting Western readers.

Tappytoon

Bringing the best of Korean webtoons to the world.

  • Founded: 2013
  • Country: South Korea
  • Known For:Solo Leveling, What's Wrong with Secretary Kim, The Abandoned Empress, Light & Shadow

Tappytoon is a premier digital-first comics and web novel platform operated by Contents First Inc., boasting a readership of over 8 million readers in 241 countries and offering the most comprehensive collection of Korean webtoons and web novels in English, French, and German. The platform had its big break with What's Wrong with Secretary Kim, and Solo Leveling was key in bringing in action fans and male readers, opening the gateway to license even more major action fantasy titles. Unlike larger platforms that rely on advertising, Tappytoon focuses mainly on a monthly subscription model spanning romance, action, BL, fantasy, drama, and GL genres, featuring both all-ages and mature content.

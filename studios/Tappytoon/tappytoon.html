Overview

Tappytoon is a premier digital-first comics and web novel platform operated by Contents First Inc., a South Korean venture-backed media and entertainment company founded in 2013 by co-founders Sun Bang and Ernest Woo. Launched in August 2016 as a curated library of premium Korean webtoon translations, it quickly established itself as one of the most trusted and polished platforms for official English-language manhwa outside of South Korea. As of 2026 the platform has grown to serve over 8 million readers in 241 countries, with its library available in English, French, and German.

Unlike open publishing platforms, Tappytoon focuses exclusively on officially licensed, vetted translations of premium titles sourced directly from Korea's biggest publishers including Naver Webtoon, Kakao, D&C Media, Seoul Media, Haksan, Comico, and Mr. Blue. This curated approach means a smaller but higher-quality library compared to competitors, earning it a reputation as the go-to destination for readers who want accurate, professionally translated manhwa.

Tappytoon had its big break with What's Wrong with Secretary Kim, which exploded globally when the K-drama adaptation aired. Solo Leveling was key in bringing in action fans and male readers, opening the gateway to license even more major action fantasy titles. In July 2021 Tappytoon launched Tappytoon Studio, an in-house division for producing original webtoons and owned IP. In 2022 Tappytoon acquired two content production studios — C&C Revolution and Maru Comics — and established a joint venture with Redice Studio, the art studio behind Solo Leveling and Omniscient Reader's Viewpoint.

History

  • 2013 — Contents First Inc. is founded by Sun Bang and Ernest Woo in Seoul, South Korea.
  • 2016 — Tappytoon officially launches in August as a curated app-based webtoon platform. Receives early funding from BonAngels. Achieves over 100,000 downloads within nine months of launch.
  • 2017 — iOS app launches. A Kickstarter campaign to publish Bloody Sweet as a physical graphic novel raises over $20,000.
  • 2018 — Ranked the number one Android Comics App in both North America and Europe and Top Five in iPhone apps in the Book category. What's Wrong with Secretary Kim becomes the platform's first major breakout hit.
  • 2020 — French and German language services launch. Tappytoon becomes the number one grossing Comics app in France on Android within two months of the French launch.
  • 2021 — Tappytoon Studio launches in July for original IP production. Webnovel service launches in August.
  • 2022 — Tappytoon acquires Maru Comics and C&C Revolution. Establishes a joint venture with Redice Studio. Surpasses 7 million registered users across 241 countries in November.
  • 2026 — Tappytoon continues expanding its library and original IP production, serving 8 million+ readers globally.

Business Model

Tappytoon operates primarily on a subscription-based model through its Tappytoon Club membership, which provides members with monthly points, daily check-in bonuses, and reduced wait times on Time Till Free series. It also offers one-time point bundles for readers who prefer not to commit to a monthly plan. Many series offer free starter episodes, after which readers use points to unlock further content.

Unlike platforms that rely on advertising, Tappytoon's revenue model is driven almost entirely by reader purchases, ensuring a fully ad-free reading experience. The platform also generates income through its studio division, producing original IP for print publication, transmedia adaptations, and licensing deals with major publishing partners including Yen Press and Inklore, an imprint of Penguin Random House.

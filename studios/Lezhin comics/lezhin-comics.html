Overview

Lezhin Comics is a premium webtoon platform operated by Lezhin Entertainment, a South Korean company founded in June 2013 by blogger Han Hee-sung and developer Kwon Jeong-hyuk. It holds the distinction of being the first pay-to-read digital comics platform in Korea, pioneering a model that demonstrated webtoons could thrive without relying on advertising revenue. The platform specializes in content for mature audiences, featuring over 8,000 titles across genres including romance, boys' love, girls' love, horror, action, and psychological thrillers.

Unlike Naver Webtoon and Kakao which built their dominance on free ad-supported content, Lezhin built its identity on premium quality and creator-first values, promising writers more money and greater creative freedom than any other platform at the time. This approach attracted many of Korea's most talented artists and writers who were drawn to the promise of better earnings and fewer content restrictions. By November 2014, just over a year after launch, it had grown to host over 400 titles and was recognized as South Korea's largest webtoon publisher at the time.

In December 2020, Lezhin Entertainment was acquired by Kidari Studio for approximately 130 billion Korean won, entering a new phase of its development under new ownership. As of 2026, Lezhin continues to operate globally with a library of over 8,000 titles and a dedicated international readership, particularly strong in the BL and mature romance genres.

History

  • 2013 — Lezhin Comics launches in June as Korea's first premium pay-to-read webtoon platform, founded by Han Hee-sung and Kwon Jeong-hyuk.
  • 2014 — Lezhin grows to over 400 titles and is recognized as South Korea's largest webtoon publisher. Receives a 5 billion KRW investment from NCsoft.
  • 2015 — Lezhin launches its Japanese language service in July and expands to English-speaking regions in December.
  • 2017 — Lezhin hits 10 million registered users. Attends Anime Expo 2017 in North America. Creator payment controversies emerge, drawing public criticism. Late penalty fee scandal surfaces.
  • 2018 — Lezhin earns 10 million USD in the US market alone. CEO Han Hee-sung officially acknowledges and apologizes for the blacklist controversy. Late penalty fees abolished.
  • 2019 — Lezhin Studio is founded as a drama and film production subsidiary to adapt webtoon IPs into films, dramas, and games.
  • 2020 — Kidari Studio completes its acquisition of Lezhin Entertainment for approximately 130 billion KRW in December.
  • 2026 — Lezhin continues global expansion with 8,000+ titles and a surging international fanbase, particularly in the BL genre.

Business Model

Lezhin Comics operates on a coin-based payment system where readers purchase virtual coins to unlock episodes directly. Unlike the free-with-ads model of Naver Webtoon, Lezhin charges readers per episode, with most titles offering a limited number of free starter episodes before requiring coins to continue. The platform also offers a membership subscription that provides monthly coin bonuses, making it more economical for regular readers who binge multiple series.

Creators on the platform are compensated through a revenue sharing model tied to episode sales, receiving approximately 30% of coin sales generated by their titles. To provide income stability, Lezhin offers a minimum monthly guarantee to its contracted creators, ensuring a baseline income regardless of readership numbers. This model was revolutionary at launch and helped attract high-quality creators who preferred the reliability of paid income over the uncertainty of ad-revenue sharing used by competitors.

Quick Facts

  • Founded: March 2, 2021 (merger of KakaoPage and Kakao M)
  • Predecessor: Daum Webtoon (2003), KakaoPage (2013)
  • Country: South Korea
  • Headquarters: Jeju, South Korea
  • Parent Company: Kakao Corp.
  • Platforms: Kakao Webtoon, KakaoPage, Piccoma (Japan/Europe), Tapas (North America)

Overview

Kakao Entertainment is one of South Korea's largest and most powerful entertainment conglomerates, operating across webtoons, web novels, music, drama production, and film. Its webtoon arm traces its origins all the way back to 2003 when Daum — one of Korea's earliest web portals — launched the world's first official webtoon platform, Daum Webtoon. That platform became Kakao Webtoon in 2021 after a series of corporate mergers and is now one of the two dominant webtoon platforms in South Korea alongside Naver Webtoon.

Kakao Entertainment was formally established on March 2, 2021, through the merger of KakaoPage Corp. and Kakao M. Beyond Korea, it operates Piccoma in Japan and Europe and owns Tapas Media in North America, giving it one of the widest international footprints of any Korean webtoon company. The company is home to some of the most commercially successful manhwa titles ever published including Solo Leveling, The God of High School, and True Beauty, and has invested heavily in adapting its IPs into Korean dramas, Netflix originals, anime, and video games.

History

  • 2003 — Daum launches the world's first official webtoon platform, Daum Webtoon. First major hit is Kang Full's Love Story with over 32 million views.
  • 2013 — KakaoPage launches as a mobile content platform under Podotree Co., Ltd., a Kakao subsidiary.
  • 2014 — Daum and Kakao merge, bringing Daum Webtoon under the Kakao umbrella. KakaoPage introduces the revolutionary "Wait-or-Pay" monetization model.
  • 2016 — Kakao Piccoma launches in Japan using the Wait-or-Pay model, rapidly growing a massive Japanese readership.
  • 2018 — KakaoPage acquires Neobazar, Indonesia's top webtoon platform.
  • 2021 — KakaoPage and Kakao M merge to form Kakao Entertainment. Daum Webtoon rebrands as Kakao Webtoon and expands to Thailand and Taiwan.
  • 2021 — Kakao acquires Radish Media for USD 440 million and Wuxiaworld for USD 37.5 million, expanding its global web fiction presence.
  • 2023 — Kakao becomes the largest shareholder of SM Entertainment, further expanding its entertainment empire.
  • 2025 — Kakao Entertainment launches Helix Shorts, an AI-powered tool that converts webtoon panels into short-form animated videos.

Business Model

Kakao Entertainment's most significant contribution to the webtoon industry is the invention of the "Wait-or-Pay" model, introduced by KakaoPage in 2014. Under this system, readers can access the latest episode of a webtoon for free if they wait a set period of time, or pay a small fee using virtual coins to read immediately. This model struck the perfect balance between accessibility and monetization and was quickly adopted across the industry worldwide.

Beyond platform revenue, Kakao Entertainment generates significant income through IP licensing and adaptation deals. The company signed over 100 adaptation deals since 2021, with titles being adapted into Korean dramas, Netflix originals, anime, and video games. In 2025 it launched Helix Shorts, an AI tool enabling creators to convert webtoon panels into short-form animated content targeting platforms like YouTube Shorts and TikTok to reach new global audiences.

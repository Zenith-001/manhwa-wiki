  • Country: United States
  • Headquarters: El Segundo, California, USA

Overview

Tapas Media, now operating as Tapas Entertainment, is a US-based digital comics and web novel platform founded in 2012 by Korean-American entrepreneur Chang Kim and Young-Jun Jang. Originally known as Comic Panda before being renamed Tapastic, it claims to be the first webtoon portal site in the English-speaking world. Kim conceived the platform as a YouTube-style model for comics — an open publishing space where creators could share and monetize their work — inspired by the success of the webtoon model already thriving in South Korea.

As of February 2021 the website reports that it hosts English-language works by over 61,000 creators. Most of the works on Tapas are in romance and fantasy genres. Around 63% of creators on its service are women and the majority of its users are women aged 18–24. Around 80% of its works are comics, while the rest are prose.

In May 2021 Tapas Media was acquired by Kakao Entertainment. On August 1, 2022, Kakao Entertainment merged Tapas Media and Radish Media to form Tapas Entertainment, a newly merged entity serving as a global super IP discovery base and K-Webtoon outpost in the North American market. As of 2026 Tapas Entertainment operates with approximately 199 employees across 6 continents and continues to be one of the most important platforms for English-language webtoon readers worldwide.

History

  • 2012 — Founded by Chang Kim and Young-Jun Jang as Comic Panda, later renamed Tapastic. Launches as the first open webtoon publishing platform in English.
  • 2014 — Receives Series A funding of $2 million USD through a partnership with Daum Communications. Begins bringing popular Korean webtoons to English audiences for the first time officially.
  • 2015 — Company receives $3.4 million USD total in backing from American and Korean investors including Daum Kakao and former Facebook CTO Adam D'Angelo.
  • 2016 — Releases a new mobile app titled Tapas and transitions to a freemium business model.
  • 2017 — Website officially renamed from Tapastic to Tapas. Right of First Refusal clause added to terms of service, sparking creator controversy.
  • 2020 — Tapas paid USD 14 million to creators over the course of the year. Users typically spent USD $50,000–$70,000 each day on the service.
  • 2021 — Kakao Entertainment acquires Tapas Media in May. Tapas hires several DC Comics veterans to develop original IP.
  • 2022 — Tapas lays off many of its DC-hired staff in a change of direction. Tapas Media and Radish Media officially merge into Tapas Entertainment on August 1. Korean office closed as part of restructuring.
  • 2023 — Founder and CEO Chang Kim departs Tapas Entertainment to pursue new ventures. Wuxiaworld absorbed under the Tapas brand.
  • 2026 — Tapas Entertainment operates with ~199 employees globally, continuing to serve as Kakao Entertainment's primary North American webtoon and web novel platform.

Business Model

Tapas works on a freemium business model where many series have the first few episodes available for free but further episodes must be purchased. In 2020, users in total typically spent US$50,000–$70,000 each day on the service, and over that year Tapas paid US$14 million to creators.

Creators on Tapas can publish through two routes — as independent creators on the open platform, or as contracted Tapas Originals with dedicated editorial support and higher revenue guarantees. The platform uses an ink-based virtual currency system where readers purchase ink to unlock premium episodes. Tapas also operates a subscription tier that provides monthly ink bonuses and ad-free reading, making it economical for regular users who read multiple series. Its merger with Radish Media expanded its web novel offerings significantly, making Tapas Entertainment one of the most comprehensive digital storytelling platforms available to English-speaking audiences in 2026.
